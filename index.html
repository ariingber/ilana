Self Love and Compassion

“The worst enemy to creativity is self doubt” – Slyvia Plath

• The Happy HeART studio infuses lessons on positive self-talk and mindfulness to awaken students’ access to their creativity.

• Every art class opens and closes with guided meditations to help students quiet self-limiting beliefs.

• At the Happy HeART studio we actively practice self-love, self-care, and positive self-talk to cheer ourselves on. We are reminded to replace our inner critic with our inner coach.

Close

Growth Mindset and Effort

“No matter what your ability is, effort is what ignites that ability and turns it into accomplishment.” – Carol S. Dweck, author of Mindset: The New Psychology of Success

• At the Happy HeART studio we learn to enjoy challenges and celebrate mistakes as opportunities for problem solving. When students make mistakes we cheer! “Yes a mistake! A chance to solve a problem!”

• Students will learn the power of the word YET. Instead of saying “I can’t do it” students will be reminded that they just haven’t gotten it “YET.”

• Students will build perseverance and learn to trust that through practice their abilities will grow.

Close

Gratitude

“Gratitude is one of the most medicinal emotions we can feel. It elevates our moods and fills us with joy.” – Sara Avant Stover

• Scientific research is showing that people who practice gratitude by taking time to reflect on things they are thankful for experience more positive emotions, feel more alive, sleep better, express more compassion and kindness, and even have stronger immune systems.

• At The Happy HeART studio we take time to practice feeling grateful for our art supplies, our abilities, our hands, our friends. We practice feeling grateful for our lives and the present moment.

• Especially when we feel frustrated with our artwork, we are reminded to insert gratitude as a tool for gaining perspective.

Close

Courage

“To live a creative life we must lose our fear of being wrong” - Joseph Chilton Pearce

• Through exploration of many different materials, artists will learn to practice courage and risk taking in their artwork.

• At The Happy Heart studio we study artists who haven taken risks and made new discoveries. We encourage artists to remain curious and to experiment like scientists to see what happens when they try something new.

• We practice creating without fear of making a mistake or making something that looks “wrong” or “bad.”

Close

Mindfulness

“Mindfulness means paying attention in a particular way, on purpose, in the present moment non-judgmentally” – Jon Kabat-Zinn

• Artists will learn to slow down and consider their choices before making artistic decisions, practicing thoughtfulness.

• As students are creating art they will be reminded to feel their paintbrush on the paper, feel their hands on the clay, and notice their present moment experience and thoughts.

• At the Happy Heart Studio we will learn breathing techniques, meditation strategies, and yoga exercises to enhance our creativity and present moment awareness.

What We Teach

Through mindfulness, yoga, and art making activities, it is our objective to foster student creativity, build self-esteem and resilience, teach children how to self-love and self care, and positively impact others to create a peaceful, compassionate, and loving world.

Our Teacher

Ilana received her masters in Art Education from Columbia University and has been certified to teach yoga by YogaWorks and Little Flower Yoga for children. She has taught at the Guggenheim, Santa Cruz School, and Success Academy acting as their Art Content Lead, providing professional development for 30 art teachers.

Bio

Ilana Ingber moved to Santa Cruz, California in 2015 from New York City. Passionate about nature, art, yoga, and mindfulness, the move to Santa Cruz was a natural fit. Ilana received her masters in Art and Art Education from Teachers College, Columbia University.

She specializes in social emotional learning and infuses themes such as: self-love, compassion, collaboration, growth mindset, positive self-talk, courage, risk taking, and gratitude in all her lessons. Ilana has taught in both the public school, private school, and museum settings. She began her career at the Solomon R. Guggenheim Museum’s Learning through Art Program and Creativity Institute. Later she taught at Success Academy Charter School for 5 years and became the Art Content Lead, providing mentorship and professional development for 30 art teachers across the network of Success Academy schools.

While teaching in the inner city, Ilana became very serious about her own meditation and yoga practice as tools for expanding her heart, practicing compassion, and managing stress. She has been formally studying and practicing yoga and meditation daily since 2011. Ilana saw so much value added to her own life that in 2015 she decided to get her 90 hour certification for teaching mindfulness and yoga to children from Little Flower Yoga. In 2016 she traveled to Fiji to receive her 200-hour teacher training with YogaWorks, a leading yoga teacher training in the field. Ilana is passionate about helping her students find inner confidence, joy, and meaning through the teachings of art, yoga, and meditation.

Ilana is an active member of the National Art Education Association and has presented at numerous national conventions on topics such as: Teaching Collaboration Skills through Painting Curriculum, Assessing Creativity, and Infusing Mindfulness Practices into the Art Studio.

Our Studio

The Happy Heart Studio is privileged to teach on Costanoa Commons Organic Farm! The farm is located across from the Tannery Arts Center. Students may create art indoors in the “art barn,” with heat, air conditioning, and shade, or create outside on the farm property.

Costanoa Commons Organic Farm 335 Golf Club Drive Santa Cruz CA 95060

Costanoa Commons Organic Farm

Happy Heart Studios is particularly excited about the farm partnership because it allows students to participate in farm beautification projects, drawing farm animals and plants from observation, as well as, gardening experiences. Additionally, the farm has a large stage where students can practice yoga in the fresh air, under a shaded tent, and surrounded by thriving vegetables.

Costanoa Commons’ vision is to create an urban farm that will offer a meaningful place for people with and without developmental disabilities from the surrounding community to work, volunteer and socialize. The community farm will offer farming skills training, employment, volunteer work, and recreation on a roughly four-acre organic farm, where high-quality food will be grown year-round.

The farm will include aquaponics, the farming of plants and fish in a symbiotic system so that wheelchair users also can farm. We envision Costanoa Commons Farm as a sustainable social enterprise, where farm products are sold to local chefs and restaurants, at our farm stand, and through our CSA and contributed to local food banks.

Eco-friendly features will be incorporated into all of our development. Relationships with the surrounding community are a high priority, offering opportunities for mutual relationships, employment, and recreation. We expect to be careful stewards of our historic parcel, including caring for its natural resources.

The property is located in Santa Cruz, CA, approximately three miles from the University of California, Santa Cruz.

More about the farm: costanoacommons.org
